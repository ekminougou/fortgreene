Preliminary findings:


Fort Green is a primarily residential community that is currently in a development boom. The neighborhood and nearby downtown Brooklyn are popular neighborhoods with many luxury developments springing up. Within Fort Greene there are three historical districts with many landmarked buildings. The historical districs include Fort Greene, Brooklyn Academy of Music, and Wallabout.

Block 2072 has 37 residential buildings with 9 mixed-use residential/commercial buildings, a transportation/utility building, and a public facility. Overall there are not many complaints for the block. The most complaints are along Myrtle Ave where there is more traffic and a few buisiness, including restaurants. One residential building has an unusual number of violations (54) and complaints (20) between 2014 and 2016. This may indicate that there are disagreements between the landlord and the tenant.

Near the block and along the north side of the park are NYCHA housing, the Whitman Houses and Ingersoll Houses. The Ingersoll site is currently a site that that is being reviewed by the city for development. An RFP has been issued to develop affordable senior housing on a small lot within the houses, alongside the park. The development should help to address some of the needs of the community and city for affordable senior housing in an increasingly expensive neighborhood.

Demographics

Fort Greene is a neighborhood with younger people and small families. According to the 2010 U.S. Census, the 99,600+ residents have an average household size is 2.06, the median household income is $71,754 (much higher than NYC ($50,400) and Brooklyn ($48,000)) the median age is 34, 45% of the population is male and 55% is female. 69% of the dwellers are renters, 15% own their own home with a mortgage and 4% own their home with no mortgage. 42% of the population within Fort Greene is African American/Black. 28% of the population is White, 18% is Hispanic/Latino, and .3% is Asian.

Location

Fort Greene is located near downtown Brooklyn and is accessible by many different transportation modes including, Citibike, 2, 3, 4, 5, B, C, F, Q, R, and G trains, as well as the B25, B26, B38, B52, B54, and B69 bus lines.

Land Use and Zoning

Most of Fort Greene is zoned to allow residential housing (R6 and R6B). There is a cluster of R7-2 medium density apartments located in the southeast corner of Fort Greene and R7-A units along the commercial corridors, Myrtle Ave and Fulton Street. C6 zoned commercial buildings are mainly located along Flatbush Avenue.

The land use map reflects and reinforces the zoning as shown above (zoning map) and below (land use map). The map illustrates how Fort Greene is mainly residential area with mixed use building lining Myrtle Avenue and Fulton Street. Some commercial and industrial building are located along the edges of the neighborhood.

The average number of floors in a building located in Fort Greene is 3 floors, the average lot size is 5,444 square feet (sqft), and the average assessed land value is $82,021. Despite the average number of floors in the neighborhood being relatively low, there is great variation in the height of buildings with the highest building rising up to 44 floors. Residential floor area (13,415,292 sqft) takes up 64% of the total building area (20,934,895). Office (1,935,350 sqft) and retail (1,520,441 sqft) make up a much smaller portion of the neighborhood at 9% and 7%.

History

1776 - Fort Greene was constructed as Fort Putname and used as a high ground during the American Revolution.
1836 - Within a year after Fort Greene is incorporated into the city, the neighborhood population grew from 5,210 to 24,310.
1867 - Newly designed by Frederick Law Omstead and Calvert Vaux
1897 - Renamed from Washington Park to Fort Greene
1905 - New crypt entrance and stairway designed by architectural firm McKim, Mead and White
1978 - Houses along Washington Park granted Landmark Status and Fort Greene is designated as a Historic District
1995 - $1,166,000 reconstruction of the northwest playground
